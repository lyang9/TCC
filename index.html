Navajo Nation


Xin

As a graduating student I had an inner debate before going on this trip, as mid-March was supposed to be a crucial time for my graduation thesis work and job searching. "Will I fall behind the progress or lose job opportunities? Can I have peace on this week-long trip?" I eventually decided that I should first give time to Him because it isn't mine in the first place. I visited the Navajo Nation with a heart determining to connect with and learn form the native people as well as Pastor Lee and his wife, who was serving as missionaries. Pastor Lee's story truly inspired me to have faith in following God's leading in my life. "I am the vine; you are the branches. If a man remains in me and I in him, he will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing." (John 5"15)








Pang

As a newborn Christian that time, I decided to join the mission trip to do morefor God and experience Him more even though I didn't know what's exactly mission trip is at that time. The Navajo mission trip lets me deeply feel how powerful God is. We visited the Great Canyon and so many different beautiful national parks, the high mountain tops and the deep canyons that were all created by God's hand. I was excited to know more about God and meet other brothers and sisters in Christ on the other side of the nation and hopefully hear some of their sharing. I am so thankful to see other people believing the same beliefs as we do. At the church in Navajo Nation, as they sing the praise songs in their own language, even though I could not understand the meaning, but I can feel the faith and happiness throughout their voice the way they praise our Lord Jesus. My whole body was shaking when I heard them sing. God is everywhere caring different kinds of people through out the world. After the trip, all I want to say is, I have more faith in God, I want to do more for Him. And it's so amazing that I never thought I could make it to this trip because of the money issue. I have prayed for it alone and with friends. And ask for Him if you think you want me to go to this mission, please make it happen. Have faith in God, He will take care of the rest. He has a great plan for all of us, just the matter of time.









Wen Qian

In my mind, my parents have always been overprotective. So before going on to mission trip, I have to go through the "mission impossible" first, which is an approval from my mom. When I asked her with my 0.00...01% of hope and 100% of fear. Somehow she agreed!! It then became my first mission trip and my first trip without the company of my parents. Sometimes, you just have to ask God an leave the rest of the work to Him. "God can do anything, you know - far more than you could ever imagine or guess or request in your wildest dreams!" (Ephesians 3:20-21) With everything prepared we arrived Navajo and met up with Pastor Lee and his wife. Pastor Lee taught us about Navajo's culture and that they are very sensitive to what we do/say With my mixed feelings of excitement and nervousness, we arrived at the New Testament Indian Gospel Church. Everything went out great! Everyone was extremely friendly! Aside from the difference of race, culture, and language we are just the same. I can deeply feel the welcome and love from them. We may be strangers but under GOD we are all brothers and sisters.
